<p><p>The automotive suspension control arm market encompasses two primary types: upper arms and lower arms. Upper arms are positioned above the vehicle's axle, playing a crucial role in maintaining the alignment of the suspension while allowing for vertical movement during driving. Lower arms, situated below the axle, support the weight of the vehicle and contribute to the overall stability and handling. Both types are essential for effective suspension systems, enhancing ride quality, steering response, and vehicle safety.</p></p>
