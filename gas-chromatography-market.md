<p><p>Gas Chromatography (GC) is an analytical technique used to separate and analyze volatile compounds in a mixture. It works by vaporizing the sample and passing it through a column filled with stationary phase material, where different compounds interact with the stationary phase at varying rates, leading to their separation. GC is widely used in industries such as pharmaceuticals, environmental monitoring, food and beverage testing, and petrochemicals due to its precision and ability to analyze complex mixtures.</p><p>The Gas Chromatography Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14.5% during the forecast period, driven by increasing demand for high-quality analytical data across various sectors. Factors contributing to this growth include advancements in technology, the development of more sophisticated instruments, and an increasing focus on environmental safety regulations. Additionally, the growing application of GC in the pharmaceutical industry for quality control and research is further boosting market expansion. Emerging trends include the integration of GC with mass spectrometry for enhanced analytical capabilities and the rise of gas chromatography in the field of environmental analysis and toxicology, reflecting its versatility and relevance in addressing contemporary analytical challenges.</p></p>
