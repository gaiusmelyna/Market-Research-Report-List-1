<p><p>Shunt reactors are essential for voltage stabilization in electrical networks, particularly in transmission systems. The market is primarily divided into two types: air-core dry type and oil-immersed type. Air-core dry type shunt reactors are lightweight, corrosion-resistant, and efficient for outdoor applications, while oil-immersed types offer superior cooling and insulation properties, making them suitable for high-capacity installations. Both types cater to different operational needs and environmental conditions, influencing their adoption in various power systems.</p></p>
