<p><p>Microbial Fuel Cells (MFCs) are innovative devices that convert organic matter into electrical energy using microbial processes. The market for MFCs is divided into two main types: Mediator and Mediator-free MFCs. Mediator MFCs utilize external compounds to facilitate electron transfer between microbes and the electrode, enhancing power output. In contrast, Mediator-free MFCs rely solely on the direct transfer of electrons from microbes to the electrode, offering a simpler design and potentially lower costs while achieving sustainable energy production.</p></p>
